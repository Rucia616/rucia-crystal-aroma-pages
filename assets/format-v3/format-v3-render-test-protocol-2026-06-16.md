## Objective

Validate which product format is most promising after upgrading the asset from illustrated concept boards to semi-real product renders.

## Core Decision

Should the first offer direction be:

- A: Crystal Aroma Stone Kit at $49
- B: Reed Room Diffuser at $49
- C: Full Room Mood Set at $79

## Why Use Render Mockups

This stage tests product-form preference. Respondents need to judge what the product is, whether it feels giftable, whether it seems fragrance credible, and whether the price feels believable.

Illustrated boards are useful for early concept language. They are too abstract for product-format choice.

## Controlled Variables

- Same scent: Clean Reset
- Same VI: Aroma Oracle / Prismatic Scent Quiz
- Same target use case: room reset / giftable home fragrance
- Same render quality and studio lighting
- Same question framing

## Visual QA Gate Before Spend

Do not run a paid survey until Rucia manually approves this board.

The board fails the spend gate if:

- One option looks noticeably more polished or cheaper than the others.
- A does not clearly show crystal aroma stone plus scent oil.
- B does not clearly show reed diffuser plus fragrance bottle.
- C looks like random clutter instead of a complete gift set.
- The text cannot be read in a normal laptop screenshot.
- The images make the product look like crystal decor rather than fragrance.

## Primary Question

Which one would you click or consider buying if you saw it in an Instagram/TikTok ad, gift guide, or online shop?

Ask respondents to explain:

- What do you think the product is?
- What feels most appealing?
- What feels confusing or hard to believe?
- Does the price feel reasonable?
- Would you buy it for yourself, as a gift, or neither?

## Recommended Sample

Low-cost pretest first:

- 20-30 US women, 18-34
- Use only if visual QA passes

Scale to 50 only if:

- The first 20-30 responses are readable and not dominated by confusion caused by the asset
- At least one format shows a real lead on click intent, product clarity, or giftability

## Pass Gates

Continue if:

- At least 65% correctly identify the options as home fragrance / diffuser / scent products.
- A or C does not fail because users doubt scent strength.
- Price pushback is specific and fixable.
- A format wins for reasons tied to product value, not just prettier packaging.

## Stop Or Revise Gates

Revise before spending more if:

- A is read as crystal decor.
- B wins only because it is familiar and A/C are unclear.
- C is described as cluttered, confusing, or too much.
- Most users do not understand how the aroma stone works.
- Users anchor A or C below $30.

## What This Test Does Not Prove

This test does not prove:

- Real scent performance
- Refill intent
- Production cost or margin
- Shipping safety
- Final packaging appeal
- Creator content performance

The next product-stage proof still requires physical scent throw testing.
