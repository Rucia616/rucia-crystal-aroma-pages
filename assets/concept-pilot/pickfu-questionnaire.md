# PickFu Questionnaire

Use this exact draft for the first concept pilot.

## Poll Type

Recommended safest first spend: Head-to-Head image poll.

Start with:

1. A vs C: `Scent Sign Diffuser Kit` vs `Room Mood Ritual Kit`.
2. Only if needed, B vs D: `Vibe-Matched Crystal Aroma` vs `Birth Chart for Your Room`.
3. Only if both rounds are useful, run the two winners against each other.

Optional faster route: Single Select image poll with 4 options, but only if the final quote is within the agreed cash guardrail.

Do not change the images between rounds.

## Audience

Primary target:

- Country: United States
- Gender: women
- Age: 18-34
- Sample size: 30 for pilot

Fallback if cost or availability is poor:

- Country: United States
- Gender: women
- Age: 18-44
- Sample size: 30

Optional targeting if available without making the panel too narrow:

- home fragrance
- candles
- self-care
- wellness
- astrology
- TikTok / Instagram shopping

Do not force niche spiritual targeting in the first pilot. We need to know whether the concept works for giftable wellness buyers, not only committed mystic buyers.

## Poll Context

Please review these four early concept boards for a new home fragrance product. The product is a flameless room diffuser kit with scent oil, reeds, small crystals, and a playful quiz that matches the customer to a scent. These are not final packages or brand names.

## Main Question

Which concept would you be most likely to click on if you saw it in an Instagram/TikTok ad, gift guide, or online shop?

Choose one option and explain why.

## Required Follow-Up Prompts

Use these as written if the platform allows multiple follow-up questions. If not, combine them into the explanation prompt.

1. In your own words, what do you think this product is?
2. What feels most appealing or clickable about the option you chose?
3. What feels confusing, too spiritual, gimmicky, or not believable?
4. Would you consider buying this for yourself or as a gift? Why or why not?
5. Optional only if the poll format allows it without raising cost too much: What price would feel reasonable for this as a giftable diffuser kit?

## Bad Poll Setup To Avoid

- Do not ask whether people "love crystals"; that over-selects mystic believers.
- Do not lead respondents with "this is a spiritual healing product"; that makes the concept heavier than intended.
- Do not show different layouts or different product amounts across options.
- Do not test brand name, price, packaging, and concept all at once.
- Do not use friends, family, or Chinese social media reactions as evidence for U.S. demand.
- Do not treat a small winning margin as a real winner.
- Do not spend above the agreed cap just because the poll builder suggests add-ons.

## Decision Rule

If respondents can describe it as fragrance/diffuser and one concept wins without high cringe/skepticism, we continue to landing and price validation. If they mostly describe it as a crystal set, astrology app, tarot kit, or wellness claim, revise before spending on samples.
